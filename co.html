Colombia Team

As students on an international research trip to Colombia, our interdisciplinary fieldwork examines small-scale farming in the Coffee region, with a specific emphasis on Cacao cultivation. We will use Geographical Information Systems (GIS) to digitize and map data, creating visualizations of crop distribution and environmental patterns related to cocoa plants. By integrating field-collected data with GIS tools, our work provides valuable insights that serve as a foundational resource for decision-making. Our research leverages computer science to develop a deep-learning-based image detection program that aids international farmers in diagnosing and managing crop diseases, using image-processing techniques and data collected from Colombia. Utilizing the GIS and image detection data, we can conduct a thorough biological analysis offering clear insights into farmers' crops and showcasing ways to optimize their crop growth. This opportunity fosters collaboration with Colombian farmers, promoting cross-cultural exchange while advancing farming techniques, utilizing GIS, and promoting sustainability.
